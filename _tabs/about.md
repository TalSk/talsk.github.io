Over the past decade, I turned my hacking hobby into a career. My interests and experience span reverse engineering, malware analysis, embedded and mobile security, web hacking, cryptography, and computational complexity. I also teach a biannual workshop on assembly, x86/x64 reverse engineering, and black-box research.

I hold an M.Sc. in Theoretical Computer Science from the Weizmann Institute, and presented my thesis at the [14th ITCS conference at MIT](https://itcs-conf.org/itcs23/itcs23-accepted.html).

Today I'm the Head of Research at [Astrix Security](https://astrix.security/) (acquired by Cisco), where I dig into how cloud providers implement connectivity between (and by) non-human identities — and how that connectivity breaks. That line of work led to [**GhostToken**](https://astrix.security/learn/blog/ghosttoken-exploiting-gcp-application-infrastructure-to-create-invisible-unremovable-trojan-app-on-google-accounts/), a 0-day in Google Cloud Platform that let a malicious app hide itself as an invisible, unremovable trojan on any Google account. I [presented it at DEF CON 31](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZAZvIEIjGYU), and have also spoken at RSAC, Reversim Summit, and assorted BSides and OWASP chapters.

I keep coming back to Pokémon GO. I was part of the 2016 "Unknown6" group that [broke its anti-cheating system](https://arstechnica.com/gaming/2016/08/anti-cheat-technology-stopped-pokemon-go-hackers-for-four-days/), and years later reverse-engineered its anti-cheat and network protocol from scratch — a journey I presented at DEF CON 33 as [*Breakin' 'Em All*](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2En96Cg9BFw) and wrote up here in [Part 1]({% post_url 2023-10-04-Hacking-Pokemon-Go %}) and [Part 2]({% post_url 2025-07-28-Hacking-Pokemon-Go-2 %}).

A few other side treks along the way:

* I hacked vehicle infotainment systems at a previous job.
* I turned a OnePlus 5T with a broken screen into an [ad blocker](https://github.com/pi-hole/pi-hole) for my home network, alongside a privacy-focused [meta search engine](https://github.com/searxng/searxng).
* I performed open-heart surgery on my (one month out-of-warranty) Nintendo Switch to replace its fan — a small nod to the ["Right to Repair" movement](https://www.repair.org/stand-up/).

This blog is where the rest lands: reverse engineering, security research, CTF write-ups, and assorted side treks.
